# Video title: How to Build in Public: 10 Examples to Copy (B2B SaaS)

Link: https://youtu.be/AKcAn8-s-cM?si=gAvPWgF7wmoqV9gt
Date: Dec 22, 2024
Channel: Jonathan Rintala (Univid)

## Key Ideas

- Argues distribution matters as much as product — recommends pushing content on LinkedIn, YouTube, and wherever the ideal customer audience is active, even in a "blue ocean" market.
- Gives 10 concrete content formats: sharing failures/screwups, sharing wins/growth numbers, weekly product updates (short-form video), sharing exclusive user/product data, personal learnings, polls/engagement challenges, sales/growth experiments (e.g., his own Reddit playbook), people met along the way, events attended, and "industry beef" (calling out competitors or opposing opinions for engagement).
- Cites named real examples: Adam Robinson (RB2B) for vulnerability/data-sharing posts, Marc Lou for engagement challenges and contests.
- Recommends starting by copying the style of creators you admire before developing your own voice, referencing the book "Steal Like an Artist."

## Why It Matters

The single most directly useful video collected so far for a YouTube/content-strategy playbook — a structured, repeatable framework (10 content categories) with named real-world examples, directly answering "what should a B2B SaaS founder actually post."
