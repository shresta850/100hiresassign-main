## Key Ideas

- Argues most YouTube channels chase virality (clickbait titles, shock thumbnails) leading to saturation; the alternative is optimizing for what Google and YouTube search already shows demand for.
- The "search-first formula": pick topics people search monthly on Google/YouTube (verified via search-volume tools) instead of guessing at viral angles — produces consistent, compounding views rather than unpredictable spikes.
- A "ranking checklist" drives search-based channels: exact keyword in the title, a description that summarizes the video with the keyword in the first lines, and timestamps/chapters for extra visibility.
- Title and thumbnail should do different jobs — title carries the keyword, thumbnail sells the click — and the target keyword should actually be spoken aloud in the video, since YouTube/Google can parse audio content directly.
- YouTube is now one of the most-cited domains in AI search overviews, meaning YouTube SEO compounds into AI-driven visibility, not just traditional search.

## Why It Matters

The most directly actionable "how-to" video collected across all experts — a complete, repeatable framework for growing a YouTube channel through search rather than virality, which is a fundamentally different (and more sustainable) strategy than the build-in-public/viral approaches seen from Marc Lou and Vasco.
