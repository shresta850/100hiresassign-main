# Video title: Inside Ahrefs' $100M Growth: Tim Soulo on SEO, Content, and Social

Link: https://youtu.be/Nw-HgjJClpM?si=BhpyTykTvZmizt8s
Date: March 18, 2025
Channel: Tim Soulo (Ahrefs) — podcast interview

## Key Ideas

- Tim personally started Ahrefs' YouTube channel himself years ago, then handed it to a dedicated creator (Sam); the channel is now reportedly the biggest YouTube channel in the SEO niche, with some videos hitting half a million views.
- Frames the shift to video as a behavioral one: people increasingly prefer watching a tool being used over reading a tutorial or review — driving Ahrefs to invest more in video for both top-of-funnel and product marketing content.
- Describes organizational structure: a YouTube team (focused solely on the main channel), a separate video team (handles landing page explainers, podcast intros, broader video needs), distinct from the blog/content team.
- Notes the limits of attribution — Ahrefs has never had clean marketing attribution data, ranking channel impact intuitively (SEO, word-of-mouth, and brand as the rough top three) rather than from precise tracking.
- Describes a parallel shift toward native LinkedIn posting (rather than just linking out to the blog) as attention has moved off Twitter/X.

## Why It Matters

The richest single piece of evidence for how a real $100M ARR B2B SaaS organizationally resources and structures YouTube as a long-term channel — not a side project, but a dedicated team with its own creator, distinct from general video production. Directly complements the SEO-tactics video with the "how this actually gets staffed and run" perspective.
