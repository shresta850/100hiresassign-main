# Video title: I Documented My SaaS Journey to $20,000 MRR

Link: https://youtu.be/UTjU_30SHIo?si=QIgN-XBiW109KCGM
Date: June 1, 2026
Channel: Marc Lou (DataFast, ShipFast, CodeFast)

## Key Ideas

- Compiles 500+ days of daily build-in-public clips chronicling DataFast's growth from $0 to $20K MRR (~$1M valuation).
- Growth was driven almost entirely by Twitter, not paid marketing — viral moments (a security bug payout, public GDPR pushback, users sharing screenshots) repeatedly brought attention back to the product.
- Documents real setbacks transparently: a scam customer, runaway hosting costs, failed features, and a multi-week public backlash over exploited paywalls.
- States explicitly the SaaS was grown "without ever doing any of the traditional marketing," relying on organic word-of-mouth and user-shared screenshots instead.
- The video series itself — publishing every milestone day, good or bad — functioned as a long-running content strategy that built a loyal audience over an 18-month arc.

## Why It Matters

Demonstrates "build-in-public" as a content format distinct from tutorial-style YouTube content. Shows both the cost (public backlash, visible failures) and payoff (sustained audience growth) of transparency over time — relevant to a content strategy playbook even though the primary acquisition channel shown here is Twitter rather than YouTube directly.
